About The Event

The combined antiretroviral treatment (ART) has helped people living with HIV to achieve almost the same life expectancy and quality of life as the general population. On the other hand, longer life expectancy also brings about other health challenges. The global HIV prevalence among adults has been estimated at around 3.6 million (1). The number of people aged 50 and above was projected at around 7.5 million by 2020 or around 21% (2). The proportion was higher than that of 2016. Asia and the Pacific regions showed the highest surge (from around 15% in 2016 to about 45% in 2020 (2). Around 80% of people living with HIV aged 50 and above lived in low- and middle-income countries. In Indonesia, it has been estimated that the proportion of people living with HIV aged 50 reached 9.2% in 2020 (3). All adults with HIV are at risk of age-related diseases, yet those aged 50 years and above have an even higher risk. It is, then, essential to support people living with HIV in facing age-related health issues and achieving healthy ageing.
